Project Overview

When the Ocean Fell Silent is an interactive projection prototype designed to help visitors understand how ocean noise pollution disrupts whale communication. Visitors’ shadows become whale silhouettes, and the system responds in real time to ambient sound: whales can communicate in quiet conditions, become disrupted under medium noise, and fragment into fossil-like outlines under high noise.

Module & Context

Module: Interaction Design (Embodied Interactions)
Format: Embodied installation (shadow + sound input)
Core idea: Turning an “invisible pollutant” (noise) into a felt, physical experience

Key Interaction Logic

  • Presence: visitor shadows appear and morph into whale forms in calm conditions
  • 3-tier noise system: low = stable, medium = disrupted communication, high = fragmentation
  • Fossil stage: the environment “stops responding” to emphasize damage
  • Recovery: after ~5–8 seconds of quiet, whales can reform (cooldown prevents instant reset)
  • Hint message: “Quiet helps the ocean heal.” appears to encourage collective quietness

Tools & Methods

  • Unity (prototype build)
  • Shadow-based embodied interaction
  • Microphone / ambient sound as real-time input
  • Iterative prototyping (visual tests, thresholds, timing)

Poppion

Final Year Project · Unity Game · Decision-Making, Waiting & Uncertainty

Overview

This final year project explores whether emotional engagement can be created through interaction structure rather than explicit narrative.

The prototype was developed in Unity and focuses on three core conditions: decision-making, waiting, and uncertainty, using recognisable young adult experiences such as academic pressure and job applications.

Research Focus

The project investigates how emotional responses can emerge from mechanics such as delayed feedback, repeated choices, and suspended outcomes.

Instead of relying on story alone, the design asks players to experience uncertainty directly through interaction.

Concept Development

Early thinking, visual planning, and initial structure exploration.

Flow & Storyboard

The project evolved from a more complex branching structure into a clearer two-chapter prototype.

Chapter One – Academic Pressure

Chapter One is structured around a repeated decision loop, where players make choices between studying, resting, and social actions without being shown clear progress.

Hidden variables and delayed consequences create tension, uncertainty, and pressure, reflecting the emotional weight of academic decision-making.

Chapter Two – Waiting & Uncertainty

Chapter Two shifts from active decision-making to passive waiting. Players move through familiar interfaces such as social media and email, where interaction becomes slower, more repetitive, and less controllable.

The chapter focuses on suspended agency, repeated checking behaviour, and the emotional uncertainty of waiting for an outcome that may never arrive.

Visual & Interface Design

The prototype uses familiar digital interfaces and a restrained visual language to keep the focus on emotional interaction.

User Testing & Iteration

User testing with eight participants helped identify how players responded to hesitation, hidden rules, repeated choices, and email checking behaviour.

Feedback informed further refinements, including pacing adjustments, clearer visual hints, and the addition of the “No New Emails” notification to distinguish intentional waiting from technical failure.

Reflection

This project showed how emotional engagement can emerge from interaction mechanics themselves, especially when grounded in familiar life experiences.

It also developed my skills in Unity, interaction design, visual planning, testing, and iterative refinement across a research-led creative project.
